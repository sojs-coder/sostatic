Exxon Mobil (XOM) Stock Analysis

Report Date: February 17, 2026 | Ticker: NYSE: XOM

Executive Summary: Exxon Mobil remains a dominant force in the global energy sector, characterized by robust cash flow generation and a strong commitment to shareholder returns. Despite a slight decline in net income in 2025 due to normalizing energy prices, the company maintains a fortress balance sheet and is strategically pivoting toward lower-carbon solutions while optimizing its core upstream assets.

1. Company Overview

Exxon Mobil Corporation is the largest U.S.-based oil and gas company and one of the world's largest publicly traded energy providers. The company operates through four primary business segments:

  • Upstream: Exploration and production of crude oil and natural gas. This remains the primary profit driver.
  • Energy Products: Refining and marketing of petroleum products (fuels, lubricants).
  • Chemical Products: Manufacturing of petrochemicals used in various industrial and consumer applications.
  • Specialty Products: High-value products including lubricants and waxes.

Recently, Exxon has expanded its Low Carbon Solutions business, focusing on carbon capture and storage (CCS), hydrogen, and lithium production to address the global energy transition.

3. Stability and Credit Profile

Exxon Mobil is recognized for its financial stability, maintaining one of the strongest balance sheets in the industry.

  • Credit Ratings: S&P Global: AA- (Stable) | Moody's: Aa2 (Stable).
  • Debt Management: The company has significantly reduced its debt load since 2020. Its net-debt-to-capital ratio remains at a healthy level, providing a cushion against commodity price volatility.
  • Dividend Reliability: A "Dividend Aristocrat" (though technically removed from the S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats index in 2020, it has continued to raise dividends for 40+ consecutive years).
